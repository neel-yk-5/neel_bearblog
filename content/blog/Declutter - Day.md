I succumbed last night to YouTube but then got back on track immediately after 2 hours and went to sleep.
This morning, I realised the main source behind my compulsion to check the phone is anxiety about new messages, emails, or any piece of information that might come to me which I am missing out on — or sometimes I even open my phone and refresh my RSS reader to check what new articles are posted. And after I get bored when nothing new pops up, we have YouTube and other distractions which pull me in, so it's not only YouTube or LinkedIn — it's the combination of that anxiety that you might be missing something and then turning to YouTube to soothe that anxiety.

So to cure this **information anxiety** -
- I am going to reduce my consumption of new information from a daily to a weekly level where I read articles on my Kindle (using ktool.io) at the end of the week, not every day
- Batch my message checking to afternoon before lunch and at night before dinner, outside of those two periods I don't keep checking my phone
- Work Communication - this one is probably harder — I haven't figured out a way to do this, but for email I can check once at the start and once at the end of the day and not in between. Messaging is trickier here; maybe I'll add focus blocks on my calendar and respond to messages outside of those blocks
- Another thing I want to try out at work is **meditate on what matters everyday by writing all the stuff you need to do before doing anything else**

My main source of information during the day will be books (non-fiction / fiction). This avoids the constant checking behaviours which I want to avoid.
